Going through the most basic, the need to knows.

    Teams

    There are two teams.

    • Terrorist ( T ) How T look like
    • Counter-Terrorist ( CT ) How CT look like

    Maps

    The maps currently in the active duty pool, and one must be familiar with.

    • Inferno
    • Nuke
    • Mirage
    • Overpass
    • Ancient
    • Vertigo
    • Anubis

    Spawns

    Spawns are where the team start their round from.

    • CT Spawn
    • T Spawn

    Sites

    These are the sites where the T, can plant the bomb.
    For a CT these are the places you hold while not knowing where the bomb is, as the T needs to get to one of these places.
    On most map CT usually place two players on each of these sites, and one person between them.

    • A
    • B

    Win conditions

    These are the normal win conditions for a single round. winning the MAP, means to first get 16 rounds.

    • Defuse

      When a bomb is planted, it can be defused, if the CT defuse the bomb they win the round.

    • Bomb

      When the T plant the bomb, a timer starts. After the timer ends the bomb is exploded and the T win the round.

    • Kill all CT/T

      During the original round timer, if all players on one team dies, they lose the round.
      During a bomb plant, as a T you win if you kill all the CT, but as a CT you need to defuse the bomb to win the round, even if you kill all the T.

    Rounds

    One half on one team, then switch at half point.
    Normally one half is 15 rounds, but the standard is to play best of 30 ( first to 16 ), so only the first half is guaranteed to play to 15 on that team(ct, t)

    Timers

    Timers are what's used most, this can change.

    • Freeze-time: 12 seconds (timer before a round start)
      it's very important to be done buying weapons when this time is over, so practice buying faster if you are not moving when the timer is up.
    • Round: 1minute 55seconds (standard round timer, if bomb is not planted)
    • Bomb timer: 45 seconds (timer til it explodes, after being planted).
    • Defuse time is 10seconds but can be 5seconds with a defuse kit.
      Usually a 10second warning music starts playing when 10 seconds remain, try to use this music to know when you still can defuse.
      (Meme: don't be a loser buy a defuser)

How to move, and where to move as a team

    T

    As a T you need to get to either A or B to plant the bomb, as said earlier, the CT usually place two players on A and two players on B,
    meaning that if as a T you walk 5 players into one site, it will usually be 5 v 2, giving the T the upper hand and making it possible to plant the bomb.
    As a T you should play together and not to spread out, try to stick with your team.

    CT

    As a CT you are most often alone, or with one team-mate. If you see the T, you need to inform your other CT players that the T is close, making them come to your help.
    If you as a CT hear a lot of noise on the communication and T getting kills on your team members, you need to move to help that area, as the T is there
    And as said earlier, the T often moves together and not spread out to far, meaning that if one T is at an area, there are more and they most likely are not where you are.
    So if you hear something, call it out and if you hear the info being called, you need to help out.

    If the T has planted the bomb, the CT need to Retake and defuse the bomb.'
    as a CT you should wait for your team to be ready to enter the site, do not just walk in alone.

The basic of nades

Smoke

The smoke grenade creates a medium-area smoke screen

The smoke is used to remove angles, lets say you walk into a corner formed as an T, if you smoke of one side, you only need to focus on the other.
As a T, try to remember from where you often die, you can use that information to understand where you should smoke.

Flash

The non-lethal flashbang grenade temporarily blinds anybody within its concussive blast

The flash is used to blind the enemy team, making the enemy not able to see anything for a second or two.
this is often used when walking forwards, if you used a smoke on the left side, and you are to walk right, but often die to a player there you can try flashing it first, then move.

Molotov

The Molotov is a powerful and unpredictable area denial weapon that bursts into flames when thrown on the ground, injuring any player in its radius

This is used to avoid enemy to stay in one position
often used during an "execute" (taking site). This is used in common angles, to not allow the enemy to stand there while you enter.
it can also be used to keep enemy off certain things, for instance the bomb. If it's 10seconds left on the timer, a molotov that covers the bomb will most often make you win the round.

Nade

Grenade that burst dmg in an small area it explodes.

The nade is used only if you have a specific plan with it, that's not often but some examples are:
Nade a common awp angle, where awp has been playing.
Nade stop rush, ( when all enemy run to a site )
Nade/molotov combo to not allow early agression.

Decoy

When thrown, the decoy grenade emulates the sound of the most powerful weapon you are carrying

The decoy is very easy to distinguish from normal shooting sounds, so it's not very much used,
but a use case can be on a pistol round, you can throw it towards A, then go B, this will create some confusion but not much.
